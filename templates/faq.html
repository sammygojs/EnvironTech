Frequently Asked Questions

How do solar panels work?

Solar panels work by converting sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic cells. These cells generate direct current (DC) electricity, which is then converted into usable alternating current (AC) electricity for your home or business.

What is the lifespan of solar panels?

The average lifespan of solar panels is around 25-30 years. However, many solar panels continue to produce electricity beyond their warranty period. Regular maintenance and proper installation contribute to the longevity of solar panels.

How can I estimate my energy savings?

You can estimate your energy savings by considering factors such as your location, the size of the solar installation, and your energy consumption. Our experts can provide a personalized estimate based on your specific circumstances.

Are there government incentives for installing solar panels?

Yes, many governments offer incentives for installing solar panels, such as tax credits, rebates, and feed-in tariffs. These incentives can significantly reduce the overall cost of your solar installation. Check with local authorities for available programs.

What maintenance is required for solar panels?

Solar panels generally require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning to remove dirt and debris, as well as occasional inspections for any damage, is recommended. Our team can guide you on proper maintenance practices to ensure optimal performance.

Can solar panels work in cloudy weather?

Yes, solar panels can still generate electricity in cloudy weather. While they are most effective in direct sunlight, modern solar technologies are designed to capture diffuse sunlight on cloudy days. However, the energy output may be lower compared to sunny days.
